The reality

Your engineering team doesn't need to hear from another executive or advisor who speaks in buzzwords and TLAs (yes, that's Three Letter Acronyms). They need someone who can spot why your CI takes 47 minutes when it should take 6. Why upgrading to the latest version of a library might actually be a mistake. Who knows which AI tool is actually useful versus expensive noise. Who can translate between complaints about "technical debt", your customers wanting a dark mode and your board asking about "velocity".

The brutal truth: Every decision compounds, architectural ones especially. From big and small, it all counts. From which feature to start building, to which company to acquire. Choose right and you will be delighting customers, with happy teams, wrong and you will be stuck watching your competitor' success from the sidelines, as you rewrite, rehire and rethink.

I've been helping teams and companies grow and thrive for 20 years. From running my own consultancy to startups like Demotix (built, scaled, acquired by Corbis), and TubeBuddy (due diligence, integration, growth),  and leading technical teams at a Bill Gates owned business BENlabs, for 12 years (scaled up, acquisitions, AI integration, long term investments).
